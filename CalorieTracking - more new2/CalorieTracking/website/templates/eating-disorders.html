Eating Disorders Overview

Anorexia

  • Serious illness, not a lifestyle choice, involving restricted eating.
  • Common Ages: 10–20
  • Signs: Skipping meals, depression, dizziness
  • Symptoms: Starvation, emotional distress, physical decline
  • Effects: Extreme weight loss, low blood counts, dehydration
  • Treatment: Therapy, supervised weight restoration

Bulimia Nervosa

  • Binge eating followed by purging or misuse of laxatives.
  • Effects: Electrolyte imbalance, gastrointestinal issues
  • Signs: Frequent bathroom visits after meals, swollen cheeks
  • Treatment: Counseling, nutritional education, medication

Binge Eating Disorder

  • Loss of control over eating large quantities of food.
  • Common Ages: 20–80
  • Signs: Eating in secret, distress over bingeing episodes
  • Treatment Tips: Meal planning, therapy, support groups
